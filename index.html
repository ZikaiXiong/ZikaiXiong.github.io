About Me

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student at the MIT Operations Research Center, advised by Robert Freund. My current research interests are mainly in optimization and operations research. I’m interested in both theoretical foundations and computational practice.

My research up to now has involved enhancing the scalability of optimization algorithms for solving large-scale linear and conic optimization -- both generically as well as on specific applications. Some of my methods have already been implemented in the state-of-art commercial solvers (COPT in particular) and are designed for newer computational architectures (like GPUs). My overarching aspiration is to extend the capability of modern optimization methods to effectively solve problems arising in operations research, machine learning/AI, and data science.

In addition to MIT, I have had research experience at JPMorgan Chase AI Research (summer 2023), and at Fudan University where I worked on projects supervised by Yinyu Ye (Stanford) and Dongdong Ge (STJU); see below for details.

I am on the academic job market in AY2024-25.
